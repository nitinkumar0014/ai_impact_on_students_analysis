# ai_impact_on_students_analysis
# Impact of Generative AI on Student Academic Performance

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the impact of Generative AI usage on students' academic performance, learning behavior, skill retention, anxiety levels, and burnout risk. The analysis combines Python for data exploration and statistical testing with Power BI for interactive dashboard development.

The objective is to understand whether AI acts as a productivity-enhancing learning tool or contributes to dependency-related challenges that may affect long-term learning outcomes.

---

## Problem Statement

Generative AI tools have become increasingly popular among students for assignments, research, coding assistance, and exam preparation. While these tools can improve efficiency, excessive dependence may influence academic performance, skill retention, and overall student well-being.

This project investigates:

* How frequently students use AI tools.
* Which academic majors adopt AI the most.
* Whether AI usage improves GPA.
* The relationship between AI dependency and learning outcomes.
* The impact of AI usage on anxiety and burnout risk.

## Statistical Findings

### Independent T-Test

Comparison:

* Low AI Users
* Heavy AI Users

Results:

* T-Statistic = 3.485
* P-Value = 0.00049

### Mean GPA Comparison

| Group          | Mean GPA |
| -------------- | -------- |
| Low AI Users   | 3.342    |
| Heavy AI Users | 3.320    |

### Interpretation

The analysis found a statistically significant difference between the two groups. However, the actual GPA difference was relatively small, indicating limited practical impact.

This suggests that excessive AI usage does not necessarily result in higher academic performance.

---

## Key Insights

* STEM students demonstrated the highest AI adoption rates.
* AI is primarily used for assignments, research, and coding assistance.
* Moderate AI usage appears more beneficial than excessive usage.
* Heavy AI users did not significantly outperform low AI users academically.
* AI dependency may influence long-term skill retention.
* Student burnout and anxiety remain important factors when evaluating AI adoption.

---

## Recommendations

* Promote responsible AI usage among students.
* Encourage critical thinking alongside AI-assisted learning.
* Provide AI literacy and prompt engineering training.
* Monitor skill retention in addition to GPA performance.
* Develop institutional AI usage policies.
* Track burnout and anxiety indicators among students.
